Abstract

This PhD thesis examines how administrative systems classify complex disability-related barriers. It argues that standardised eligibility processes can misrecognise fluctuating conditions.

Chapter 1 Introduction

The research question asks how disabled claimants describe the gap between formal assessment categories and lived experience.

Chapter 3 Methodology

The thesis uses qualitative interviews, policy analysis, and thematic coding.

Chapter 6 Findings

The findings suggest that procedural simplification may improve consistency while also suppressing minority cases.
